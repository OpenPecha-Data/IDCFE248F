PRE FACE
In this volume of the Smanrtsis Shesrig Spend zod we have
the pleasure to present to scholars an extremely important and rare
Nyingmapa text, 'Gyur-med-tshe-dbang-mchog.grub's detailed
commentary on a little Nyingmapa Lam-rim text from the gter-ma
rediscoveries of Nyang-ral Nyi-ma-'od-zer.
The Gelukpas are usually regarded to be the masters of
the scholastic Lam -rim approach. Here the reader will find a system
atic presentation of Nyingmapa concepts and constructs. Another
Nyingmapa Lam -rim (the Ye shes snang ba of Kong-sprul) has already
appeared. The present work is an earlier and more detailed work.
The author, 'Gyur-med-tshe-dbang-mchog-grub or Kah
thog Dge-rtse Pandita belongs to the late 18th century. Our text
suggests that he was born in about 1764. The present work was
written in 1805. The par-byang is dated 1824 and mentions the
patronage of the ruler of Sde-dge, Sa-dbang Rin -po-che, for carving
the blocks. The author of this par-byang would seem to be Dge-rtse
Pandita himself. Since he mentions that he was 61 years of age in
1824, one can date tenatively his birth to about 1764. The blocks
were kept at Lcags-la (Dar-rtse-mdo).
There is an example of this work in the Sorensen collection
(no. 157) in the Oslo University Library'. Several of the texts
preserved there are Lcags-la prints; the associations of Lcags-la
with Rdo-rje-brag were a result of the birth of one of the Rdo-brag
Rig-'dzin-chen-mo incarnations in the ruling house of Lcags-la.
1. A catalogue of the Sorensen collection with a biographical
sketch of Theo Sorensen by Per Kvaerne will soon appear under
the Manjusri imprint.

possible that this text contains an autocommentary attributed
to Padmasambhava. In any case, the otsa-ba which goes with
the Dge-rtse commentary was not available here in India. The
